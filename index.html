What we know about The Pirate Bay aka TPB

The Internet continues to develop at an unprecedented speed, but one thing remains unchanged in it — this is The Pirate Bay.


Swedish file sharing service. One of the world's largest BitTorrent portals, which allows users to exchange music, movies, books and computer programs, is considered one of the most important elements in the global movement in support of piracy and anti-copyright. He became famous in 2006, after the Swedish police commandeered the servers on which the site was located, but three days later he started working again on servers in the Netherlands, and a few weeks later he returned to Sweden. In April 2009, the court found the creators of The Pirate Bay guilty of aiding copyright infringement and profiting from piracy through advertising on the site. They were sentenced to a fine and one year in prison. In November 2009, it was announced that the BitTorrent tracker was being closed, and users would download files using tracker-free technology.
